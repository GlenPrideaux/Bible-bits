Esther
The Septuagint (LXX) has sections of Esther that are not found in the standard Hebrew copies (the MT). There is also another family of manuscripts called the Alpha Text that is different again.

Samuel, Saul and David (SSD)
The Masoretic Text (MT) for the books of Samuel is famously problematic with many demonstrable errors. This edition is translated from a group of Greek and Hebrew copies that can be shown to be faithful (called the Palestinian Text). (The Old Greek of the LXX has its own issues, but most of the readings of this edition will match those of the Septuagint.)

Jeremiah
Jeremiah is unusual in that in the time of Jesus and the apostles it existed in two distinct forms with essentially the same message but with different ordering and emphases. The version that comes to us in our English bibles is based on the longer Hebrew Masoretic Text (MT) version, but the version quoted by the apostles and prophets of the New Testament is the shorter Greek Septuagint (LXX) version which is in turn based on a corresponding Hebrew version that was known in the first century but unknown in modern times until its rediscovery in the Dead Sea Scrolls. Scholars think the Septuagint version represents the older textual tradition that has been expanded on in the MT, but almost all modern editions translate from the MT, although they may include notes as to the differences. This document presents Jeremiah in LXX order using the 1870 Brenton English translation in parallel with the World English Bible (WEB). Verse numbering follows Brenton.
